# Bill of Materials (BOM)

Here is the list of components required for the SmartNexus-MA prototype. These components are selected to be low-cost, widely available, and easy to assemble for a student project.

## Core Components
| Item | Description | Quantity | Estimated Cost |
|------|-------------|----------|----------------|
| **ESP32 Development Board** | ESP32-WROOM-32 (30 or 38 pins). Main microcontroller with WiFi. | 1 | $5 - $8 |
| **Solar Panel** | 12V, 20W to 50W (depending on pump size and sunlight availability). | 1 | $20 - $40 |
| **Solar Charge Controller** | 10A PWM Solar Charge Controller (12V/24V auto). | 1 | $10 - $15 |
| **Battery** | 12V 7Ah Sealed Lead-Acid (SLA) or LiFePO4 equivalent. | 1 | $15 - $25 |
| **DC Water Pump** | 12V Submersible DC Water Pump. | 1 | $10 - $15 |

## Sensors & Modules
| Item | Description | Quantity | Estimated Cost |
|------|-------------|----------|----------------|
| **Soil Moisture Sensor** | Capacitive Soil Moisture Sensor v1.2 (Corrosion resistant). | 1 | $2 - $4 |
| **Water Flow Sensor** | YF-S201 (1-30 L/min, 5V logic). | 1 | $4 - $6 |
| **Voltage Sensor** | 0-25V Voltage Divider Module (for measuring 12V battery). | 1 | $1 - $2 |
| **Current Sensor** | ACS712 (5A or 20A version) or INA219 (I2C, more accurate). | 1 | $3 - $5 |
| **Relay Module** | 1-Channel 5V Relay Module with Optocoupler (or IRLZ44N MOSFET module). | 1 | $2 - $4 |
| **Buck Converter** | LM2596 Step-Down Module (Drops 12V to 5V for ESP32). | 1 | $2 - $3 |

## Wiring & Hardware
| Item | Description | Quantity | Estimated Cost |
|------|-------------|----------|----------------|
| **Breadboard / Perfboard** | For prototyping the circuit. | 1 | $3 - $5 |
| **Jumper Wires** | Dupont cables (Male-to-Male, Male-to-Female). | 1 set | $3 - $5 |
| **Power Cables** | Thicker AWG wires for solar, battery, and pump connections. | 2 meters| $3 - $5 |
| **Inline Fuse Holder** | With 5A or 10A blade fuses for battery protection. | 2 | $2 - $4 |
| **Terminal Blocks** | For secure wire connections. | 1 set | $2 - $3 |
| **Water Tubing** | PVC or silicone tubing matching the pump outlet. | 2 meters| $3 - $5 |

**Total Estimated Cost:** $85 - $150 (Depending on solar panel and battery size)
